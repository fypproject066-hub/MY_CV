Introduction

I'm a computer engineering student with a deep curiosity about how things work and how technology can be used to solve meaningful problems for humanity. Raised in the village of Dhamtour at the foothills of the Himalayan mountains, I developed an early fascination with understanding systems, often taking apart toys and repairing household devices simply to explore their inner mechanisms.

A strong belief in discipline and self-development has always shaped my journey. Alongside my academic interests, I have pursued activities such as gymnastics, martial arts, swimming, and reading, building both physical and mental resilience. These habits formed the foundation of my approach to learning: curiosity, persistence, and a willingness to explore complex ideas.

My academic focus lies in computer engineering and emerging technologies, particularly in areas where classical computing intersects with the rapidly evolving field of quantum science. This interest is reflected in my Final Year Project, Augmentation of Quantum Technologies with Cutting Edge Classical Technologies, which explores the potential of integrating advanced classical systems with developing quantum technologies.

Driven by a long-standing ambition to contribute something meaningful to the world, I continue to explore challenging ideas, new technologies, and interdisciplinary research with the hope of advancing knowledge and creating solutions that can benefit humanity.
